GCSE chemistry

What is an electron? (in short)

An electron is a sub-atomic particle that orbits around the nucleus.
Electrons have a negative charge (1-)

What is an electron? (in long)

Positioning of electrons

Electrons orbit around the nucleus in certain rings which are called shells.

At GCSE level the number of electrons in each shell is as follows: 2, 8, 8, 8, 8, 8, 8, 8.

This means the first shell can contain a maximum of 2 electrons. The second to eighth row can also only contain 8.

This isn't strictly true but it is a quick and easy rule that works for GCSE.

Electrons and stability

In order for atoms to become stable they must have a full outer electron shell.

This can be done in one of three ways:

  • Already having a full outer shell ( noble gases )
  • Covalent bonds bonds
  • Ionic bonds
