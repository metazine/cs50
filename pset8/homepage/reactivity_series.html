The Reactivity Series

The reactivity series is the order of how reactive different metals are. The least reactive metal is platinum. Platinum can only react at high temperatures with very strong acids. The most reactive metal is francium. Francium reacts explosively in water, and has a half life of just 22 minutes.

The reactivity is series is important in electrolysis. This is because more reactive elements can displace less reactive elements.

Example:

Ores are generally an oxide. For example iron ore (hematite) is Fe₂O₃.

If we react this with the more reactive element aluminium the iron is "displaced". This means that the iron has been reduced and the aluminium has become oxidised. This leaves you in theory with pure iron and oxidised aluminium.

Fe₂O₃ + 2Al ➔ Al₂O₃ + 2Fe

The order of reactivity.

↓

Decreasing order of reactivity

↓

  1. Francium - Fr
  2. Caesium - Cs
  3. Potassium - K
  4. Sodium - Na
  5. Calcium - Ca
  6. Magnesium - Mg
  7. Aluminium - Al
  8. [Carbon - C]
  9. Zinc - Zn
  10. Iron - Fe
  11. Tin - Sn
  12. Lead - Pb
  13. [Hydrogen - H]
  14. Copper - Cu
  15. Gold - Ag
  16. Platinum - Pt

This shows that the element Magnesium could displace zinc for example - but it couldn't displace sodium.

You may also notice two non metal elements in the list highlighted in red. These are often added to the series as they can be used to extract metals.
